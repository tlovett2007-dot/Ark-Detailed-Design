Constitution used steam bending for its 7" planks. The Ark uses cold water soaking only — limiting plank thickness to 5" (see plank bending section). Constitution's single-layer planking is replaced by cross-laminated multi-layer system — thinner individual planks, superior structural performance as box girder flanges.

2. Plank Bending Specification

Bow Radius

Beam = 25.9m Bow radius = 2/3 × beam = 2/3 × 25.9 = ~17.3m

Maximum Plank Thickness for Cold Water Soaking

Cold water soaking plasticises wood fibres — softer than steaming but avoids the burns and complexity of steam boxes. Cold bend limit (pine): Radius / 125 = 17,300mm / 125 = ~138mm = ~5.4" Hot water soaking limit: Radius / 100 = 17,300mm / 100 = ~173mm = ~6.8" Steam bending limit: Radius / 60 = 17,300mm / 60 = ~288mm = ~11.3" Selected: Cold water soaking only → maximum plank thickness = 5" (127mm) ✓ Margin: 127mm vs 138mm limit — tight but passes. Taper at bow reduces stress further.
Why cold water only: Hot planks are dangerous and impractical to handle at the scale of Ark construction. Steam boxes require industrial infrastructure. Cold water soaking in a clay-lined pit or pond is accessible pre-industrial technology. The 5" plank thickness is therefore determined by the bending constraint, not arbitrary choice.

Fitting Procedure

Cold water soaking is for bending only — not for tight fitting. Correct procedure:

StepDetail
1. Soak plankCold water immersion 1–2 weeks — fibres plasticise
2. Bend to formBend wet plank to hull form, clamp in place
3. Partially dryAllow plank to dry to near-equilibrium moisture content before fastening
4. Fit with caulking gapLeave deliberate 2–3mm gap between adjacent planks for caulking
5. FastenTreenails and/or bronze fastenings through plank and frame
6. CaulkDrive zopissa-impregnated fibre into tapered seam gap
7. Pitch overHot pine pitch poured over caulking — seals and waterproofs
The shrinkage direction: Planks fitted dry will swell in service, compressing caulking — the traditional and correct method. Planks fitted wet will shrink on drying, opening joints. The cold water soak is for bending only; fitting is done at near-dry condition with correct caulking gap.

Cross-Lamination Advantage

Each successive plank layer is laid at an alternating grain angle to the previous. This means no continuous seam runs through the full skin thickness — all layers must fail simultaneously for a leak to penetrate. The cross-lamination is a redundant waterproofing system independent of caulking quality in any single layer.

4. Frame Chocking — Dense Hardwood Wedges

Material

PropertyRequirementCandidate materials
Density>1,000 kg/m³ (sinks when dry)Lignum vitae (~1,300 kg/m³), ironbark (~1,100 kg/m³), pre-flood equivalent
HardnessMaximum — resists crushing under compressionLignum vitae (Janka ~4,500 lbf), ironbark (~3,500 lbf)
ShrinkageMinimum — driven wedges must stay drivenDense hardwoods have low shrinkage relative to volume
Self-lubricatingAids driving — reduces splitting riskLignum vitae (natural waxes), resinous hardwoods

Function

Tapered wedges driven from alternating sides between frames, driven until full resistance — creates compressive pre-stress in the frame assembly. Like pre-stressed concrete: the joint is already in compression before any wave load arrives. Wave loads must overcome the pre-stress before any gap can open.

As timber seasons and shrinks slightly, wedges can be re-driven from inside — the system is self-maintaining. Re-driving accessible wedges is achievable by crew during the voyage.

Pre-Flood Availability

Lignum vitae is today native to the Caribbean — post-flood geography. Under pre-flood Pangaea-equivalent continental configuration, ultra-dense hardwood equivalents almost certainly existed near the build site. The specification is functional (SG >1.0, maximum hardness, low shrinkage) not species-specific.

5. Caulking — Zopissa System

Historical Precedent

Pliny the Elder (Natural History, 79 AD) describes zopissa — a combination of pitch and beeswax used in Greek shipbuilding — as the standard waterproofing material. Analysis of a 2,200-year-old Roman wreck (Ilovik-Paržine 1, Croatia, ~150 BC) confirmed zopissa was actually used, along with other organic sealants.

Zopissa Components

ComponentSourceFunction
Pine pitch (resin)Pre-flood pine trees — tapped resinPrimary waterproofing, rot resistance, adhesion
BeeswaxPre-flood bee coloniesFlexibility — prevents pitch cracking in temperature cycles; extends working temperature range
Fibrous fill (oakum equivalent)Hemp, flax, or similar fibrous plant materialDriven into tapered seam gap — compressible core that swells in service; stiffens hull by wedge action

A bronze pot over controlled fire in the stern service compartment. Pre-cut fabric strips stored dry alongside. Pitched fabric is the universal repair material throughout the voyage — superior to any modern equivalent in wet conditions:

6. Bronze Spider Straps

Purpose

At major timber junctions — particularly where longitudinal laminate members meet transverse frames — conventional timber joinery is insufficient for fully reversing wave loads over 371 days. Bronze spider straps provide ductile moment connections that absorb both tension and compression alternately without loosening or fatigue failure.

Specification

ElementSpecification
AlloyHigh-tin malleable bronze — ~14–18% tin content
Why high tin14–18% tin: good castability + maximum cold-working ductility. Allows hammer-forming around irregular timber junctions without cracking. Micro-yields under reversing loads rather than fracturing.
FormCast spider shape — central hub, 4–6 radiating arms
Hub diameter~150mm
Arms~50mm wide × 10mm thick × 300mm long
InstallationCast to approximate shape → hammered cold around actual timber junction to conform exactly → steel nail through each arm into timber
SealingPitched fabric wrap over completed strap

Metallurgical Precedent

Genesis 4:22 — Tubal-Cain, "forger of all instruments of bronze and iron" — lived four generations before Noah. High-tin bronze metallurgy was current and sophisticated at the time of construction. The specification is within established pre-flood capability.

Future Exhibition Proposal

A full-scale structural demonstration section — genuine hardwood frames, dense hardwood wedge chocking, real cast and hammer-formed bronze spider straps, pitched fabric sealing — would be the first physical realisation of this hull technology at full scale. Not a replica: the actual specified construction method, built with the specified materials, touchable and demonstrable. Proposed for Ark Encounter or similar institution.
