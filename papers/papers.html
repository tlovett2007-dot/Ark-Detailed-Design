Paper 1 — "Noah Didn't Build an Ark"

ElementDetail
StatusCandidate — argument developed
Target journalJournal of Creation (AiG) / Answers Research Journal
TopicBiblical Hebrew word study — tebah, Septuagint translation, box-Ark argument
ProvocationDeliberately mischievous title to draw attention — the argument is that the word translated "Ark" carries no shape information whatsoever

Core Argument

The Hebrew word tebah (תֵּבָה) appears only twice in Scripture:

ReferenceObjectMaterialShape
Genesis 6 — Noah's vesselSeagoing vesselGopher wood300×50×30 cubits (given explicitly)
Exodus 2:3 — Moses' basketInfant's basketBulrushes (papyrus reed)Round/oval — determined by reed weaving

The Septuagint (LXX) translates both as kibotos (κιβωτός) — commonly rendered "chest" or "box" in English. This has generated the "box Ark" argument: if tebah = kibotos = box, then Noah's vessel was box-shaped.

Why the Box Argument Fails

ProblemArgument
Moses' basket is not rectangular Bulrush/papyrus reed construction produces a round or oval vessel by the nature of the material. If tebah means rectangular box, Moses' basket was rectangular — which is physically impossible for woven reed construction. Therefore tebah cannot mean rectangular box. QED.
Septuagint kibotos is functional not geometric Kibotos describes a protective container — a vessel that preserves its contents. It is a functional description (what it does) not a geometric one (what shape it is). The Ark of the Covenant is also kibotos — and nobody argues the Ark of the Covenant's shape from this word.
tebah is a functional descriptor Tebah is best understood as something like "vessel of preservation" or "lifeboat" — describing purpose not shape. It carries no geometric information. The shape of Noah's vessel comes exclusively from the explicit dimensions of Genesis 6:15 (300×50×30 cubits) — not from the word tebah.
The Egyptian coffin argument Some have proposed tebah derives from Egyptian dbt (coffin/chest) — reinforcing the box shape interpretation. But a coffin connection reinforces the FUNCTION (preservation of the body/occupant) not the geometry. Egyptian coffins were not exclusively rectangular — many were anthropoid (body-shaped). The functional parallel (vessel that preserves life through death/judgment) is theologically apt; the geometric parallel is not implied.
The Irving Ark reduction If tebah shape = Moses' basket shape, then Noah's Ark was round — an Irving/coracle Ark. This is plainly absurd given the explicit dimensions. The shape argument from tebah therefore proves too much — it produces a conclusion (round Ark) that nobody accepts, demonstrating the argument's invalidity.

The Positive Case

Genesis 6:15 provides the only shape information given for Noah's vessel: 300×50×30 cubits. These proportions (L:B:D = 10:1.67:1) are optimal for:

ParameterAssessment
StabilityHong et al. (1994) confirmed optimal stability at these proportions
SeakeepingLength/beam ratio appropriate for passive wave behaviour
Structural efficiencyDepth/beam ratio provides adequate section modulus for longitudinal bending
VolumeSufficient for the stated cargo without excess construction effort

The proportions are those of a well-designed seagoing hull — not a rectangular box barge. The word tebah is silent on shape; the numbers speak clearly. "Noah didn't build an Ark" — he built a ship. The English word "Ark" (from Latin arca — chest) has imported the box shape into translations where the Hebrew original carries none.

Paper 3 — The Tilt-Weir Pump

ElementDetail
StatusCandidate — concept developed
Target journalNaval architecture or engineering history journal — broader audience than creation science
TopicNovel passive water elevation device using vessel pitch/roll motion — sequential overflow cells, no valves, no moving parts

Novel Contribution

The tilt-weir pump uses vessel motion to transfer water upward through a series of open troughs with incrementally higher overflow walls — no valves, no moving parts, no crew operation. Wave-powered pumps using overflow principles exist (Jobb Wave Pump, 2009 patent) but use fixed coastal installations. The tilt-weir pump uses the vessel itself as the oscillating element driving internal sequential overflow cells — no prior art identified for this specific application.

ParameterSpecification
Drive mechanismVessel pitch/roll — no external power
Moving partsNone
Minimum operating angle~8° (sea state 5)
Elevation per step~200mm at 8° with 2m wide troughs
Total elevation achievable~13m (ballast zone to roof level)
Construction materialsTimber troughs + pitched fabric liner — pre-industrial technology
Prior artNone identified for vessel-motion-driven internal sequential overflow pump

Paper 4 — kopher vs hemar

ElementDetail
StatusCandidate — argument sketched
Target journalJournal of Creation / biblical studies journal
TopicTwo Hebrew words for pitch/bitumen reflecting genuine material and geological distinction

Core Argument

Genesis uses kopher (כֹּפֶר, root kaphar — to cover/atone) for Ark caulking and hemar (חֵמָר, root hamar — to boil up/be turbulent) for Babel mortar (Genesis 11:3) and Valley of Siddim tar pits (Genesis 14:10). These are not synonyms — they describe genuinely different materials:

kopherhemar
MaterialPre-flood pine resin pitch — biological, organicPost-flood petroleum bitumen — geological
FormationLiving tree resin — immediateRapid petroleum formation from flood-buried organics under post-flood geothermal conditions
ContextSalvation — Ark constructionRebellion (Babel) / defeat (Siddim)

Laboratory experiments confirm rapid petroleum formation (George Hill, 1972: coal in 6 hours; Argonne National Laboratory: coal from wood in 36 weeks at 150°C) — the post-flood geothermal conditions from "fountains of the deep" provide the necessary heat, pressure, clay catalyst and oxygen exclusion for petroleum formation within the Babel timeframe (100–200 years post-flood per Ussher chronology).

The lexical distinction in Genesis reflects real geological history: kopher describes a pre-flood biological product; hemar describes a post-flood geological product that did not exist before the flood buried the organic material that became it.

Paper 5 — Hebrew Deck Terminology

ElementDetail
StatusCandidate — argument developed
Target journalJournal of Creation / biblical studies
Topictachtiyyim/sheniyyim/shelishim — the asymmetry of "Lowers" vs ordinal counting implies middle-deck entry door

Novel Contribution

Genesis 6:16 uses tachtiyyim (Lowers — positional/spatial) rather than ri'shon (Firsts — ordinal) for the bottom level, while sheniyyim (Twos) and shelishim (Threes) are ordinal. This asymmetry — one positional term + two counting terms — suggests the reference point is not the bottom but the entry door at middle deck level. Everything is described relative to that reference: Lowers (below it), Twos (one above), Threes (two above). Architecturally confirmed: middle-deck entry is optimal for loading above waterline with practical gangway geometry. Engineering confirmed: royal cubit deck heights are only comfortable (3.4m clear) with the 524mm REC — short cubit gives marginal headroom that barely works, further supporting the long cubit.

Paper 6 — Genesis 7:20 as Draft Specification

ElementDetail
StatusCandidate — argument developed
Target journalJournal of Creation
Topic"15 cubits above the mountains" = Noah reporting his known draft, not measuring water depth above peaks

Novel Contribution

The standard interpretation (water depth above mountains = 15 cubits) requires Noah to have measured the depth above submerged mountain peaks — for which he had no mechanism. The alternative: Noah reported minimum clearance equal to his known draft of 15 cubits (7.86m at REC), the only depth measurement he could actually make. The volume tally displacement calculation independently gives ~7.5–7.9m draft — convergence from two completely independent starting points supporting both the draft interpretation and the REC cubit simultaneously.

Paper 7 — The Rooster Chronometer

ElementDetail
StatusCandidate — concept only
Target journalJournal of Creation / potentially broader science audience
TopicFree-running circadian clock of domestic fowl as timekeeping system in sealed vessel — explains precision of Genesis 7–8 calendar dates

Novel Contribution

The Genesis flood narrative records specific calendar dates (17th day, 7th month etc.) requiring continuous accurate timekeeping in a sealed vessel with no sky visibility. Clean fowl (minimum 14 roosters per Genesis 7:2) have free-running circadian clocks accurate to ±15 minutes over 24 hours, maintained without light cues, with flock synchronisation self-correcting individual drift. Accuracy over 371 days: ±1–2 days — consistent with Genesis calendar precision. The rooster contingent provided involuntary but accurate daily time signals audible throughout all three decks. Petrine typology: Noah's roosters (marking each day of judgement and salvation) anticipate Peter's rooster (marking the moment of denial at the ultimate judgement and salvation event) — Peter himself explicitly connects Noah's flood to salvation (1 Peter 3:20–21).
