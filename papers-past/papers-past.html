What Hong et al. Established

Nine KRISO research scientists applied professional naval architecture methodology to the biblical dimensions. The study was headed by Dr. Seok-Won Hong, Principal Research Scientist, BS/MS Naval Architecture, PhD Applied Mechanics — and notably, an evolutionist. This makes the findings particularly significant as an admission by antagonistic witnesses.

Hull 9 Paradox

Hull 9 (shorter, wider: L/1.5 × 1.5B × D) consistently outperforms the biblical Ark in stability and structural safety under Hong's confused sea assumption. This appears to undermine the biblical proportions — until weathervaning is considered.

ConditionHull 9Biblical Ark
Confused sea (Hong assumption)Superior — better stability and strengthGood but not optimal
Organised following sea (weathervaning)Poor — short hull broaches more easilySuperior — length provides wave averaging and pitch stability
Genesis 8:1 drying windVulnerable beam-on if weathervaning failsDesigned for this condition

The biblical proportions are optimised for an organised wind-driven sea — not a confused sea. Hong's confused-sea assumption explains why the Ark appears sub-optimal in his analysis. The length is the weathervaning vessel's greatest asset in following seas — and Lovett (2024) below explains this in detail.
